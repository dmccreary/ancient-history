## Motivation

The initial 254-concept draft for *Ancient History: Origins to 1200 CE* relied on canonical mid-2000s textbook content. The user flagged that several decade-old discoveries were missing — Göbekli Tepe, Doggerland, recent hominin finds — and that, more broadly, the concept list reflected pre-2010 archaeological consensus rather than the current decade's evidence base. We expanded the list in four passes to bring the substrate up to current academic understanding *before* freezing it for dependency-graph generation.

## Footgun pattern named during the session

**The "settled timeline" footgun** — concept lists generated by LLMs default to a textbook canon frozen in their training data. The damage is silent (no error), easy to trigger (any naive prompt), and delayed/invisible (students read a 1990s prehistory and don't know what's missing). Structural fix: review the concept list against current-decade discoveries *before* the dependency graph is built, not after.

## Revisions made

### Pass 1 — Recent hominin evidence (IDs 255–260)

User question: had recent paleoanthropological evidence been added beyond the canonical Australopithecus / Homo Habilis / Homo Erectus chain?

Added six concepts capturing post-2010 finds and earlier-than-Australopithecus taxa:

- **255 Sahelanthropus** — ~7 mya, candidate earliest hominin
- **256 Ardipithecus** — ~4.4 mya, bridges chimp-human ancestor and Australopithecus
- **257 Paranthropus** — robust australopithecine parallel branch
- **258 Homo Naledi** — Rising Star Cave 2015; small-brained hominin alive only ~250 kya
- **259 Homo Floresiensis** — Flores "hobbit" (2004)
- **260 Jebel Irhoud Finds** — Morocco 2017; pushes *H. sapiens* origin to ~315 kya

### Pass 2 — Cave Art expansion (IDs 261–268)

User feedback: "Cave Art" as a single node is too thin for art and symbolism teaching.

Added eight concepts to expand the Paleolithic visual culture story:

- **261 Cave Painting** — the practice itself
- **262 Pigment Technology** — ochre, manganese, charcoal
- **263 Hand Stencils** — globally distributed motif
- **264 Lascaux Cave** — iconic French site, ~17,000 ya
- **265 Chauvet Cave** — oldest sophisticated European art, ~32,000 ya
- **266 Sulawesi Cave Art** — Indonesia, ~45,500 ya — globally oldest figurative art
- **267 Lion-Man Sculpture** — Hohlenstein-Stadel, ~40,000 ya — earliest figurative sculpture
- **268 Blombos Engravings** — South Africa, ~73,000 ya — earliest abstract symbolic marks

### Pass 3 — Recent paradigm-shifting archaeology (IDs 269–279)

User feedback: explicitly asked for Göbekli Tepe and similar finds.

Added eleven concepts covering the post-2010 archaeological revolution:

- **269 Göbekli Tepe** — Turkey ~9600 BCE; monumental religion before agriculture

- **271 Tas Tepeler Complex** — umbrella for the ~12-site SE Turkish Neolithic complex
- **272 Pre-Pottery Neolithic** — period frame these sites established
- **273 Misliya Cave** — Israel, earliest *H. sapiens* outside Africa (~180,000 ya, 2018)
- **274 Madjedbebe Site** — Australia ~65,000 ya (2017); peopling of Sahul pushed back
- **275 White Sands Footprints** — New Mexico ~22,000 ya (2021); peopling of Americas pre-LGM
- **276 Ancient DNA Revolution** — post-2010 methodological transformation
- **277 Yamnaya Migration** — Bronze Age steppe expansion, Indo-European spread
- **278 LIDAR Archaeology** — method revealing thousands of hidden Maya/SE Asian structures
- **279 Aguada Fenix** — Mexico 2020; largest/oldest known Maya monumental complex

- **284 Kilwa Kisiwani** — Swahili coast Indian Ocean port
- **285 Sanxingdui** — bronze-culture China; major new pits 2019–2022
- **286 Liangzhu Culture** — Neolithic hydraulic state, UNESCO 2019
- **287 Erlitou Site** — candidate Xia-period state, central China
- **288 Caral-Supe Civilization** — Peru ~3000 BCE; earliest American urbanism
- **289 Cahokia** — Mississippian center, peak ~1050–1200 CE (fits course timeframe)
- **290 Chaco Canyon** — Puebloan complex ~850–1150 CE (fits course timeframe)

### Pass 5 — Yamnaya cultural and linguistic legacy (IDs 295–296)

User feedback: a single Yamnaya node was too thin given its outsized cultural and linguistic legacy.

Added two concepts:

- **295 Proto-Indo-European** — reconstructed parent language spread by Yamnaya; ancestor of Greek, Latin, Sanskrit, Persian, Celtic, Germanic, Slavic
- **296 Steppe Pastoralism** — cultural/economic engine (horse, wagon, dairy mobility) that *enabled* the migration

## Resulting graph structure improvements

- **Africa, Americas, and recent China** now have first-class concept coverage rather than being implicit footnotes
- **Climate causation** (4.2 Ka, LALIA) gives the graph explicit causal nodes for civilizational collapses
- **Methods nodes** (Ancient DNA Revolution, LIDAR Archaeology) become reusable across multiple eras
- **Yamnaya story** (Steppe Pastoralism → Yamnaya → PIE) connects Bronze Age population history to existing classical-world nodes (Sanskrit, Greek, Latin)
- **Pre-Pottery Neolithic** as a node lets Göbekli/Karahan/Tas Tepeler connect coherently and inverts the old "agriculture → religion" narrative
