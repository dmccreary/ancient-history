# Classical Empires of Asia and Imperial Networks

## Summary

With the Axial Age intellectual foundations in place, this chapter covers the great classical empires of Asia and the trade and bureaucratic networks that connected them to the Mediterranean. It treats Qin and Han China, the Maurya Empire and Ashoka's patronage of Buddhism, the Gupta Empire and Hindu synthesis, Sanskrit literature, Indian mathematics, the Parthian and Sasanian Persian empires, the Silk Road and Roman–Indian Ocean trade, and the early spread of Buddhism along these networks. It also introduces comparative imperial concepts (imperial bureaucracy, tribute systems, frontier strategy) that students can apply across all classical empires, and covers the Bantu Migrations as a parallel large-scale population and technology movement.
